THE PROBLEM

Freelancers and consultants often struggle with clients paying late, which not only causes financial stress but also disrupts cash flow and reduces available time for new projects. Late payments can lead to missed payroll deadlines, inability to cover expenses like rent or supplies, and the constant anxiety of when that invoice will actually land in your account.

WHAT YOU'LL HAVE WHEN YOU'RE DONE

✓ An automated system that sends polite payment reminders for you — no manual emails needed.
✓ Integration with a secure payment gateway to accept payments directly from invoices.
✓ Customizable invoicing templates tailored to your business needs.
✓ Real-time tracking of invoice status and payment details.
✓ Automated follow-ups for late payments, ensuring no invoice is forgotten.

WHAT YOU NEED BEFORE YOU START

You will need:
- A Stripe account (free) — sign up at stripe.com
- A product or service you offer that can be invoiced. If you haven't already set this up on Stripe, you'll do so now.
- Basic information about your clients, such as their email addresses and payment methods.

STEP-BY-STEP SETUP

1. Create a Stripe Account (Click "Sign Up" in the top right corner of the Stripe homepage)
   - After clicking Sign Up, fill out the required fields: your name, email address, phone number, and business name.
   - Check the box for "I agree to Stripe’s Terms of Service."
   - Click "Create Your Account." You will be redirected to a confirmation page.

2. Verify Your Identity (Click “Verify” on the dashboard)
   - On the Dashboard, click the "Account" tab in the top menu.
   - Click "Verify your identity" and follow the steps to upload ID documents or bank statements.
   - Once verified, Stripe will allow you to accept payments securely.

3. Set Up Your First Invoice (Click “Invoices” on the left sidebar)
   - On the Dashboard, click the "Invoices" tab in the top menu.
   - Click “Create an invoice.” Fill out the required fields such as customer name, email, and product/service description.
   - Add line items for each service or product. Use the dropdown to choose pricing and quantities.
   - Set a due date and payment terms if needed.

4. Customize Your Invoice Template (Click "Settings" in the top-right corner)
   - Click the gear icon in the top-right corner of your screen, then click “Settings” from the dropdown menu that appears.
   - Go to the “Invoicing” tab on the left sidebar.
   - Customize your invoice template by adding or removing sections like your logo, company information, and payment instructions.
   - Click "Save" at the bottom right. You will see a green "Changes saved" message.

5. Send Your First Invoice (Go back to “Invoices”)
   - Navigate back to the Invoices tab on the left sidebar.
   - Click “Send” next to the invoice you just created.
   - Stripe will send an email to your client with the invoice attached and a direct link to pay online.

6. Set Up Automated Reminders (Go back to “Invoices”)
   - Navigate back to the Invoices tab on the left sidebar.
   - Click "Actions" next to the same invoice, then choose “Send reminder.”
   - Stripe will send an automated email reminding your client about their outstanding payment.
   - You can customize this email in the settings if needed.

7. Monitor Your Payments (Go back to the Dashboard)
   - Return to the main Dashboard.
   - Click "Invoices" again to see a list of all invoices sent and their statuses.
   - Stripe will update you on when payments are made, refunded, or returned.

WHAT TO DO IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG

- If you can’t log in: Check that your email address is correct. You might need to reset your password by clicking the "Forgot password?" link at the login page.
- If invoices aren't generating correctly: Double-check all fields are filled out and that you have verified your identity on Stripe.
- If payments don't process: Make sure your bank account or card details are up-to-date. You can update these in the “Account” settings.
